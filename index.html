Heyo, I'm Tracey! Iâ€™m , and I live in St. Louis, Missouri. I first became interested in programming when I discovered Harvard's free online course, CS50. CS50 was challenging, and I love-hated it, mostly because "programming" resonated with me, but C did not! I put CS50 on hold when I recalled a really interesting course on JavaScript I had tried several years back on Khan Academy. Not liking Khan Academy's setup, I instead revisited CSS, HTML, and JavaScript using Codecademy and FreeCodeCamp, and I fell in love.

Wanting to try something a bit more structured, I ended up enrolling in a Web Design course at my local community college. There, I learned a lot of skills I would have likely missed on my own. That layed the foundation for my self-learning, and from then on I've been making my own way by utilizing awesome free resources such as FreeCodeCamp, Udemy, library textbooks, and, most importantly, Google and StackOverflow! What helped me grow the most, however, was always having in the back of my mind a unique project I was striving to build that solved a real-life problem for me ("Bookshelf", if you'd like to take a look!).

Nowadays, I create responsive, mobile-first applications that are accessible for everyone. I like to focus on projects that are functional and useful, aiming to make them beautiful by utilizing modern design techniques in all of my projects. I'm constantly learning and growing my skillset because web development is not just a career choice but also my favorite hobby. Mostly, I hope to build applications that make mundane, everyday things, automatic and interesting so life can be a little easier for everyone.
